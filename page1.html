The Data

What Does The Data Tell Me?

The data set consists of the place of usual residence of individuals living in Australia separated by into age groups. This data is collected to provide information to the public and private sectors to improve Sydney's housing and social implications. From this data, it can be seen to correlate to housing cost, where young adults are unable to afford housing in suburbs and would rent apartments in the city to be closer to work. Young families looking for housing with more space have had to move further west in areas due to the cheaper housing and new urban development. This implicates that CBDs are planned to be high density due to work opportunities and the suburbs are lower density to allow space for families.

Public Sector

Department of Employment

This department is responsible for national policies and programs that help Australians find and keep employment and work in safe, fair and productive workplaces. The department can utilise this data along with unemployment data to target areas with large numbers of 25-64 year olds occupying to ensure effective awareness in those areas.

Department of Education and Training

They are responsible for the national policies and programs that help Australians access quality and affordable early child care and childhood education, school education, higher education, vocational education and training, internation education and research. The department can utilise this data to ensure optimal distribution of their education and training centres. Also, the information of areas with an influx of new residents could prompt new facilities that could be planned for in advance.

Private Sector

Television Advertising
